SPIRE PHOTO BOOTH

For Foto Master

Foto Master is a software and hardware company in the entertainment industry, this new
type of photo booth with two shooting modes, front photoshoot and top photoshoot using a
green screen, created a new kind of object and interactions for venues, wedding receptions
to bar-mitzvah parties.
The product comprises a capacitive touch foil applied on a special glass coupled with a
DSLR camera that captures the user image two or video connected to the main CPU, which
displays interactions and animations on two monitors.
With two end-users in mind, the operator/owner and the party guest. For the first, we need to
create a quick change between shooting modes and a light, durable, and foldable booth for
a single person operator. For the party guest, we wanted to create a noticeable design.

Main processes: ​Sheet metal, ​CNC, ​Vacuum forming
Main Materials: Aluminum, Steel, ABS
Collaborators: ​Dan Eliash

DESIGNED TO FAIL

Investigative thesis project

Over time, furniture worn down loses their function and returns to be raw materials.
in their "second life" they are getting a new character.
The project examines the scale between 'standard' and 'improvised', and failure as an
opportunity in the life of the furniture. What are the opportunities that failure creates?
Can failure bring to the world a new useful object? This is a summary of research in material
failure: bending, tearing, breaking.
touch of one material with another, which will fail first? How will it look?
The project deals with the design of failure. it uses the potential of allowing
objects to be flat in their "first life", from the factory, to the store, and up to the customers.
it deals with the choreography of failure, the destruction that gives birth to the object.
complex object trapped inside surface. One bending causes a chain reaction,
a new object is born out of the surface. this way the furniture forms: stool, table and bar stool.

Main processes: ​3D Tube cutting​, ​Woodworking
Main Materials: Aluminum, Plywood, Oakwood
Collaborators:​ -

GREEN MILK

Five fingers furniture

The green milk crate of Tnuva, the largest milk products company in Israel, became iconic in
the Israeli material view. Its purpose is to transfer milk bags across Israel.
Is that “well engineering” made it resistant, so as popular among the “five fingers discount” products.
You can find the “Green Crate” anywhere: in the back of the plumber pickup, in the local pub used as a stool,
on the back of a bicycle as a carrier basket, hanged on a wall in the kids’ room as a shelf, and more.
Only obvious that a product that is already “owned” by half of the Israeli population should go another step,
to become proper domesticated furniture.

Main processes: ​​CNC
Main Materials: Plywood
Collaborators:​ Dan Eliash
